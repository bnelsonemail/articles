# Engineering Judgment

## Welcome to Engineering Judgment

When I began my engineering career more than 25 years ago, most
infrastructure decisions were built on experience, engineering
calculations, design standards, and conversations with operators who
knew their systems better than anyone else.

Those fundamentals haven't changed.

What has changed is the amount of information available to us.

Today's utilities collect enormous amounts of operational data from
pumps, pressure sensors, flow meters, storage tanks, SCADA systems,
inspections, and asset management platforms. At the same time, advances
in statistics, predictive analytics, and software have given engineers
new ways to understand how infrastructure behaves over time.

I've found myself increasingly drawn to that intersection.

After spending more than two decades designing water and wastewater
infrastructure, I began studying statistics, Python, data analysis, and
predictive analytics. Not because I believe data can replace engineering
judgment, but because I believe it can strengthen it.

The most difficult engineering decisions have never been purely
technical.

They're decisions about uncertainty.

* Should this asset be replaced now or monitored for another five years?
* Is this pressure fluctuation simply normal system behavior, or nearly an indication of something more significant?
* Is the additional construction cost justified by lower long-term operational risk?

Those are engineering questions.

Calculations provide part of the answer. Data provides another part.
Experience provides the context to understand both.

To me, that's where engineering judgment truly begins.

This newsletter was created to explore that idea.

Future editions will examine topics such as:

* Water and wastewater infrastructure
* Hydraulic engineering
* Infrastructure reliability
* Asset management
* Capital planning
* Predictive analytics
* Engineering software
* Practical applications of statistics and data analysis
* Lessons learned from real-world engineering projects

In addition to introducing new topics, I'll occasionally revisit earlier
articles with expanded analysis, updated perspectives, and lessons
learned. Engineering is a profession of continuous learning, and some of
the best ideas become stronger through reflection, discussion, and new
experiences.

Whether the discussion is about lift station reliability, force main design, asset management, hydraulic analysis, or predictive modeling, the purpose will remain the same:

To explore how engineering judgment, operational data, and predictive analytics can work together to help utilities make better infrastructure decisions.

If these are questions that interest you, I hope you'll join me and subscribe to my newsletter as we continue exploring where traditional engineering and modern analytics meet.

Welcome to **Engineering Judgment**.
