# Turning Historical Project Costs into Engineering Knowledge

One of the most common questions in engineering is also one of the more difficult to answer:

> What did a similar project actually cost?

Whether evaluating a lift station upgrade, a force main replacement, a water main installation, or a treatment plant improvement, engineers, utility managers, and planners often look to historical projects for guidance.

The challenge is that the information usually exists somewhere, but rarely in a form that is easy to access.

Cost data may be scattered across bid tabulations, engineer's estimates, project reports, spreadsheets, PDFs, procurement documents, and agency archives. Over time, organizations accumulate large amounts of valuable historical information, but turning that information into actionable knowledge can be surprisingly difficult.

As a result, answering what should be a simple question often becomes a time-consuming exercise in inter-office coordination with other Project Managers, searching through old files, and relying on institutional knowledge.

## The Gap Between Data and Knowledge

Most utilities already possess years, and sometimes decades, of project cost information.

The problem is not the absence of data.

The problem is accessibility.

Imagine being able to ask questions such as:

* What have similar lift station projects cost over the past five years?
* How have force main installation costs changed over time?
* What unit costs have been observed for 24-inch water main construction in comparable projects?
* Which historical projects most closely resemble the project currently being planned?

These questions are often answerable in theory, but obtaining the answers can require significant effort.

In many organizations, valuable cost information remains trapped inside documents rather than organized in a way that supports analysis and decision-making.

## Why Cost Query Pro Began

Cost Query Pro began as a personal software project focused on addressing this challenge.

The goal is straightforward:

> Create a platform that can organize, search, and analyze historical project cost information in a way that is useful to engineers and infrastructure professionals.

Rather than treating project costs as isolated spreadsheets, the idea is to build a structured database that allows relationships between projects, bid items, quantities, and costs to be explored more effectively.

At its core, the project is less about software and more about improving access to engineering knowledge.

## Looking Beyond Traditional Searches

One area that particularly interests me is the intersection of infrastructure analytics and artificial intelligence.

As cost databases grow, traditional filtering and reporting tools become increasingly valuable. However, there may also be opportunities to interact with engineering data more naturally.  By leveraging natural language processing and machine learning techniques, it may be possible to create a more conversational interface for exploring project costs.

For example, instead of manually building queries, users could potentially ask questions such as:

* What projects similar to this lift station have been completed recently?
* What is the typical cost range for this type of work?
* Which bid items contribute most significantly to project cost?

The long-term vision is to make historical engineering information easier to explore, understand, and apply.

## Early Development

The project is still in its early stages.

Current development has focused on building the foundation required to manage real-world engineering data, including project tracking, bid item management, data validation, testing, and spreadsheet-based data ingestion.

While much of that work happens behind the scenes, it is laying the groundwork for future analytical capabilities.

## Closing Thoughts

Engineering organizations generate enormous amounts of valuable information throughout the life of a project. Unfortunately, much of that knowledge becomes increasingly difficult to access as time passes.

Cost Query Pro is an attempt to bridge that gap by transforming historical project data into a resource that can be searched, analyzed, and ultimately used to support better infrastructure decisions.

The software itself is only part of the story.

The larger goal is helping engineers spend less time searching for information and more time applying it with a solid and defensible understanding of project costs.
