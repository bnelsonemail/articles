# The Future Engineer Will Still Need Engineering Judgment

Artificial intelligence is changing engineering.

There's no question about that.

AI can summarize reports, analyze large datasets, identify patterns, generate code, and even help develop preliminary designs faster than ever before.

Those are powerful tools.

But they don't change one fundamental truth:

**Engineering judgment remains the engineer's responsibility.**

An AI model can estimate the probability of a water main failure.

It cannot decide whether that prediction justifies replacing a critical transmission main serving a hospital.

It can identify a pressure anomaly in SCADA data.

It cannot walk into a pump station, recognize the sound of a failing bearing, or understand why an experienced operator is concerned despite what the data suggests.

The future engineer won't spend less time thinking.

They'll spend less time searching for information and more time evaluating it.

The tools will change.

The responsibility won't.

That's why I believe the engineers who thrive over the next decade won't be those who ignore AI or blindly trust it.

They'll be the ones who combine engineering judgment with modern analytical tools to make better decisions.

Engineering begins with calculations.
Infrastructure analytics powers better decisions.
