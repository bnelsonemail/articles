# Content Strategy

This topic will be developed as two separate pieces of content intended for different audiences and purposes.

---

## Website Article

### Purpose

Provide a detailed technical discussion of the engineering, operational, and economic considerations associated with force main profile decisions.

### Audience

* Civil engineers
* Utility engineers
* Utility managers
* Asset management professionals
* Infrastructure planners

### Approach

The website version will use a hybrid approach that combines:

* Engineering judgment
* Lifecycle cost considerations
* Conceptual cost evaluation
* Illustrative break-even analysis
* Utility operations perspective

The article will not attempt to establish universal design criteria. Instead, it will present a structured framework for evaluating the tradeoffs between remaining deep and installing additional air release valves.

Where appropriate, conceptual examples and representative cost assumptions may be used to demonstrate the evaluation process.

### Key Objectives

* Demonstrate engineering decision-making methodology
* Highlight lifecycle cost considerations
* Introduce structured decision-support concepts
* Create evergreen technical content
* Serve as a reference for future articles involving asset management and infrastructure analytics

---

## LinkedIn Article

### Purpose

Provide a concise discussion that introduces the design question and encourages professional discussion.

### Audience

* Engineers
* Utility professionals
* Project managers
* Industry connections

### Approach

The LinkedIn version will focus on the central question:

> At what point does it become more economical to install an additional air release valve rather than maintain a deeper force main profile?

The discussion will briefly touch on:

* Excavation costs
* Dewatering costs
* Maintenance of Traffic (MOT)
* ARV lifecycle costs
* Utility operational considerations

Detailed calculations, assumptions, and evaluation frameworks will be omitted in favor of readability and engagement.

### Key Objectives

* Share a practical engineering decision
* Generate professional discussion
* Demonstrate engineering experience
* Drive interested readers to the full website article

---

## Relationship Between the Two Articles

The LinkedIn article will serve as a high-level summary of the topic.

The website article will provide the detailed technical discussion, evaluation framework, and lifecycle cost considerations supporting the conclusions.

Readers interested in a deeper exploration of the topic will be directed to the website article for additional detail.
