# The Hidden Cost of Avoiding High Points in Force Mains

## Introduction

In a recent force main design, utility crossings created several localized high points. The design team faced a common question: should the force main remain deeper to avoid the high points, or should the profile return to grade and rely on additional air release valves?

The hydraulic implications were relatively straightforward. The more difficult question was economic.  

In the world of wastewater management, force mains play a crucial role in transporting sewage from one location to another. A common practice in the design and maintenance of these systems is to avoid high points in the pipeline, as they can lead to issues such as air pockets and reduced flow efficiency. However, this approach may come with hidden costs that are often overlooked. In this article, we will explore the implications of avoiding high points in force mains and the potential consequences it may have on the overall performance and maintenance of the system.
